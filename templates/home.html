A study on how people evaluate facial trustworthiness, emotional expression, and side-of-face differences.

  4. Complete two short questionnaires

Purpose of the study

This study examines whether naturally occurring differences between the left and right sides of a face influence how trustworthy a person appears, and whether those impressions are related to perceived femininity and emotional expression.

Why this matters

This study also connects to a broader interest in balance and perception. In Zero Point terms, balance refers to the idea that people often respond differently to signals of stability, tension, softness, intensity, or imbalance—sometimes within a fraction of a second. By studying how people judge trustworthiness, emotional expression, and facial laterality, this project may help clarify how subtle visual cues shape first impressions and how people interpret balance or imbalance in the human face.

Before you begin

You will review an informed consent page before any study tasks begin. Participation is voluntary, and you may stop at any time. Responses are collected anonymously.

About This Project

This project explored how people judge trustworthiness, emotional expression, and left-right facial differences in the human face.

Users view facial images, rate first impressions, compare facial sides, and complete a short interactive sequence.

Originally developed as a research study, it is now presented here as an exploratory experience.
